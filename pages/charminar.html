Charminar

Hyderabad’s grand four-minarets monument (built 1591)

The Charminar is Hyderabad’s most recognizable landmark, erected in 1591 CE by Mohammad Quli Qutb Shah. Its name—literally “Four Minarets”—comes from the four soaring towers that frame a magnificent central arch. Situated at the crossroads of history, trade, and culture, the Charminar anchors the old city and the bustling Laad Bazaar.

Historical Significance

  • Commissioned in 1591 CE by the fifth Qutb Shahi ruler after founding Hyderabad.
  • Believed to mark the end of a local plague and the beginning of a new capital city.
  • Stands at the heart of the old city near Mecca Masjid and Laad Bazaar.

Architectural Highlights

  • Indo-Islamic style with Persian influences; built in granite and lime mortar.
  • Each minaret rises to about 56 m and contains a spiral staircase.
  • Four grand arches form a square plaza below a mosque at the upper level.
  • Delicate stucco ornamentation and balustraded balconies provide rich detailing.

Interesting Facts

  • “Char” means four; “Minar” means minarets—hence the name.
  • Even today it is a center of trade for bangles and pearls.
  • Night illumination showcases the façade and arches dramatically.
  • The surrounding precinct remains one of India’s most vibrant old quarters.

Plan Your Visit

Location

Charminar Rd, Ghansi Bazaar, Hyderabad, Telangana

Best Time

November to February for pleasant weather; evenings for lighting.

Tips

  • Explore Laad Bazaar for bangles and street food.
  • Carry water and wear comfortable footwear.
  • Check local guidelines for access to upper levels.
